About

My work combines molecular biology, computational methods, and clinical medicine. I currently work as a project researcher at the UEF Bioinformatics Center, where I build and run multi-omics analyses for various research projects, and consult on over twenty studies annually. In parallel, I work as a local analysis support researcher for FinnGen at FIMM, analysing data from more than 500,000 Finnish participants across 2,400 disease endpoints and 45 years of health registry follow-up.

I did my doctoral work at the University of Jyväskylä, where I conducted the first comprehensive microRNA screening in Lynch syndrome — a hereditary colorectal cancer predisposition — and developed a serum miRNA-based risk prediction model using pre-diagnostic samples from healthy mutation carriers. That combination of wet lab and computational work is something I have carried into everything since.

What I find most interesting is the clinical translation problem: how molecular and population-scale data connects to questions that are actually relevant and actionable in healthcare. That means thinking about what a health system can realistically implement, not just what is statistically detectable. It is why large-scale registry data like FinnGen appeals to me, and why network medicine is where I want to go next.

Research Interests

Quantitative Genetics

Population-scale association studies and the genetic architecture of complex disease, with a focus on clinically relevant endpoints.

Multi-omics Integration

Combining genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic data to build a more complete picture of disease mechanisms than any single modality provides.

Machine Learning in Biomedicine

Predictive modelling on high-dimensional biological data, particularly for risk stratification and clinical outcome prediction.

Clinical Research

Designing studies and analyses that address questions relevant to real healthcare settings.

Risk Prediction

Developing and validating models that translate molecular or genetic signals into risk estimates.
